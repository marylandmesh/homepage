About

What are we doing here?

The Internet is broken. In the United States, the entire Internet infrastructure is owned by an oligarchy of corporations which charge obscene prices for some of the lowest speeds and data-caps in the developed world. Websites and advertizements go to great lengths in order to track users, and private communications are sold to bring you "personalized ads."

That's not how it should be.

Read more

The Internet gives every person a place free from censorship and judgement. The Internet does not require you to submit your age, race, sex, faith, or sexual orientation to every website you visit. It does not require that you conform, and it does not require that you be silent. It gives every person a voice which cannot be silenced. We are trying to build an internet where that cannot be changed - an internet where you cannot be spied on, or charged huge amounts of money for spotty, censored, ad-riddled connections, or where the service you pay those huge amounts of money for can be shut off without warning or explanation. We are trying to create an internet in which every person depends only on their neighbors, not on national or multinational corporations, for their connection.

That's why we're building a meshnet.
