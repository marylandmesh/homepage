About

What is a meshnet?

A meshnet is a network in which users connect with each other, rather than being dependent on a "tree" of providers. This means that a meshnet depends on collaboration between neighbors, and anyone connected can have access.

What is "Hyperboria?"

Hyperboria, also known as the meshnet, is the name given to the network central to Project Meshnet. It is a meshnet with over three hundred members—and counting—spread across the world, including Maryland. It uses the open-source software CJDNS to connect its members, both through the existing Internet and via physical wireless (and wired) links. Together, we make up Hyperboria.

Hyperboria is free—in every sense of the word. There is no subscription fee, and there are no restrictions on what you can say or who you talk to.

Why create an alternative?

Our objective is to create a versatile, decentralized network built on secure protocols for routing traffic over private mesh or public internetworks independent of a central supporting infrastructure.
